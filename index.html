What is OpenStreetMap?

OpenStreetMap or OSM is a free, editable street level map of the world. The data is contributed by community of users full of diverse backgrounds, interests and goals.

History of OSM

OpenStreetMap was founded in 2005 by Steve Coast. He was frustrated that large government-run, tax-funded projects like the Ordence Survey had huge datasets but did not distrubute them in a free, easy manner.

On August 22nd, 2006 the OSM Foundation was etablished to encourage the growth, development and distrobution of OSM and its data.

History of OSM continued...

JOSM, the offline editor ws created on Jan 22, 2006 and on December 4th, 2006 Yahoo! ariel imagery was allowed to be traced.

Now there are 5 million registered users with 1 million of them contributing to the map.

Learn more about OSM's history on the OSM Wiki.

OSM Organization

OSM is run by the not-for-profit OSM Founation (OSMF). OSMF has members who elect a board of directors who are response for:
  • Acting as the entity for the OpenStreetMap Project
  • Keeping the servers running,
  • Fund-raising to support the project
  • Support and communicate with working groups

OSM + Humanitarian/Dev

The importance of OSM in humanitarian aid and development was made clear by the 2010 Haiti earthquake. The data generated in the earthquake's immediate aftermath quickly became the most authortative available.

Builing on this momentum the Humanitarian OpenStreetMap Team (HOT) was encorporated.

OSM + Humanitarian/Dev II

With the success of HOT following Haiti many international organizations recognized the value of OSM. Overtime the RedCross, Peace Corps, MSF, The Department of State, and USAID developed programs to map and use OSM data.

YouthMappers

Founded in 2014 by Texas Tech, GWU, WVU and the USAID/GeoCenter

Connects university based open mapping clubs to training and projects that benefit USAID's development/humanitarian interests but also provides meaningful skills and expereince to students.

From the early days of OpenStreetMap it was anticipated that open, free map data would be a tremendous benefit for humanitarian aid and economic development. The idea was proven during the Haiti earthquake in 2010. HOT was incorporated in the immediate aftermath, August 2010, as a U.S. nonprofit, and then became a registered 501(c)3 charitable organization in 2013. Anyone is welcome to contribute to the HOT mission via our Tasking Manager. All you need is an OpenStreetMap Username. While you participate, we ask that you do your best to uphold the code of conduct of our community. See our HOT Community Code of Conduct.
